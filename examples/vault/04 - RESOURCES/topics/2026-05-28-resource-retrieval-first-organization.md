# Why Retrieval-First Organization Beats Topic-Based Filing

A filing system organized around how you store things optimizes for the moment of capture. A retrieval system organized around how you find things optimizes for every future moment of use.

The filing-first trap: you create a folder called "Ideas" because that is what the note is at capture time. Six months later you are looking for a specific idea and cannot remember if you filed it under Ideas, Projects, Business, or a daily note. The folder name encoded the moment of capture, but it tells you nothing at the moment of retrieval.

Retrieval-first means every structural decision answers one question: when I search for this note in the future, what will I know about it? The PARA structure answers this with four retrieval axes: type of content (NOTES), active project (PROJECTS), ongoing area (AREAS), or what it is about (RESOURCES).

## Key Tension
The habit of organizing "logically" by topic is exactly wrong. Topics are what notes are *about* — they say nothing about how you will need them. The right axis for organization is how you will retrieve it: by time (NOTES), by project (PROJECTS), by responsibility (AREAS), or by subject (RESOURCES). These map to how humans actually search, not how taxonomists would catalog.
