# Privacy Is the Real Value of Local AI

The common pitch for local AI focuses on cost (free) and availability (offline). Both are real, but neither is the right frame. The actual value is that your thinking — your half-formed business ideas, your assessments of people, your strategy before it is ready to share — stays on your hardware.

When you send a prompt to a cloud AI, that conversation exists on infrastructure you cannot audit. Even with data protection agreements, the conversation is physically somewhere else. For the daily intellectual work of a knowledge worker — mulling over sensitive decisions, working through difficult interpersonal situations, developing unfinished strategy — this matters more than the capability gap between local and frontier models.

The right reframe: local AI is not a cheaper version of cloud AI. It is a different privacy boundary. Cloud AI is for the work you would share. Local AI is for the thinking you would not.

## Key Tension
Most knowledge workers have accepted that their AI conversations are not private. This has become the default assumption. Local AI rejects that default — not as a political statement but as a practical choice about where your unfinished thinking lives. The question is whether the capability tradeoff is acceptable for the work you actually do most often.
