# Deliberate Practice Requires Immediate Feedback on Errors

Improvement in any skill domain requires not just repetition but repetition with immediate feedback on what went wrong. Practice without feedback optimizes for fluency, not accuracy. The expert and the amateur often spend the same number of hours practicing, but the expert's hours include corrective feedback loops that the amateur's do not.

## Key Tension
This contradicts the popular "10,000 hours" framing. The hours do not produce expertise — the feedback structure does. Two people with 10,000 hours of practice can have completely different skill levels depending on whether their practice included feedback. This reframes the question: not "how long did you practice" but "how much of your practice included rapid feedback on errors?"
